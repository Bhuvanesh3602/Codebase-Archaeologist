# Internal Operations & Risk Assessment Report: AeroGo Acquisition

**Date**: May 2026
**Prepared by**: Department of Operational Risk & Logistics
**Status**: Internal Reference Document

---

## 1. Regulatory Hurdles & FAA Timeline
Our engineering audits suggest the executive proposal's assumption of FAA type certification by June 2027 is highly optimistic:
*   eVTOL regulations are still in draft stages. Standard certification times for novel aircraft hulls average **4 to 6 years**.
*   A realistic commercial launch date is **Q3 2030**, not Q4 2027.
*   Zoning permits for vertiports on parking structures face intense local resident opposition due to noise complaints and safety ordinances.

---

## 2. Labor Markets & Pilot Shortages
The strategy team's assumption of commercial helicopter pilots working for $75/hour is inaccurate:
*   Due to international commercial airline pilot deficits, qualified multi-engine rotary pilots command standard wages of **$180 to $220/hour**.
*   AeroGo’s recruitment pipeline has identified only 3 certified test pilots, while launching California flights requires at least 45 pilots to meet shift rotations.

---

## 3. Battery Degradation & Operating Expenses
*   **Battery Lifecycle**: Lithium-metal battery packs degrading under rapid charging require complete replacement every **600 flight hours** (approximately every 10 months under standard utilization). Each replacement pack costs **$45,000**.
*   **Hull Insurance**: Actuarial partners refuse standard aviation premiums. Insurance for eVTOL hulls is estimated at **$120,000 per aircraft annually**—representing a 400% variance from the business model budget.
*   **Grid Capacity**: Charging 12 eVTOL vehicles simultaneously requires 3.6 megawatts of power. Most target vertiports do not have connection access to high-voltage grid lines, requiring local utility upgrades costing up to $1.2M per terminal.
