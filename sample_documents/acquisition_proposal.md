# Strategic Acquisition Proposal: AeroGo Air Taxis

**Date**: June 2026
**Author**: Executive Strategy Team
**Classification**: Strictly Confidential

---

## 1. Executive Summary
We propose the acquisition of **AeroGo**, a pre-revenue electric Vertical Takeoff and Landing (eVTOL) startup, for **$450 million in stock**. AeroGo has developed a prototype air taxi designed to transport passengers across dense urban areas, bypassing road congestion. 

Our goal is to launch commercial operations in 3 major metropolitan areas by **Q4 2027**, projecting a valuation multiple expansion that will yield a **$5 billion business by 2030**.

---

## 2. The Product & Technology
*   **The eVTOL Aircraft**: AeroGo's vehicle features a 4-passenger cabin, battery-electric propulsion, and a cruise speed of 150 mph.
*   **Safety Multiple**: The aircraft uses redundant fly-by-wire controls.
*   **Moat**: AeroGo holds 12 patents covering its thermal battery management and rotor blade acoustics, making it quieter than traditional helicopters.

---

## 3. Financial Forecast
We anticipate aggressive capital injection followed by exponential growth:

| Metric | Year 1 (2026) | Year 2 (2027) | Year 3 (2028) | Year 4 (2029) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Active Fleet** | 0 (Prototype) | 12 (Pilot) | 80 | 350 |
| **Capital Expenditures** | $85M | $180M | $320M | $150M |
| **Total Revenue** | $0 | $4M | $48M | $210M |
| **Net Income** | -$120M | -$195M | -$85M | $32M |

We will fund the first three years of capital expenditures and operational losses through a combination of corporate debt and secondary equity offerings.

---

## 4. Market & Demand Assumptions
*   **Pricing**: We will charge $120 per seat per trip (average distance of 15 miles). This compares favorably to premium ground ridesharing.
*   **Utilization**: We assume each eVTOL vehicle will run 12 trips per day, with an average load factor of 75% (3 out of 4 seats occupied).
*   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**: We estimate CAC at $15 per customer, utilizing high-impact social media marketing and airport billboard campaigns.
*   **Regulator Clearance**: We assume the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) will grant type certification for our eVTOL by June 2027, allowing commercial flights immediately.

---

## 5. Operations & Execution Plan
*   **Vertiports**: We will lease roof access on existing downtown parking garages to build landing hubs ("Vertiports"). 
*   **Pilots**: While the long-term roadmap targets autonomous flight, initial launches will require FAA-licensed commercial pilots. We will recruit helicopter pilots at a standard wage of $75/hour.
*   **Expansion Rate**: We will expand from 1 vertiport in San Francisco to 20 vertiports across California and Texas within 18 months of launch.
