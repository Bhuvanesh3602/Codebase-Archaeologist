# Red Team Agent

### An AI system that attacks your strategy before the market does.

---

## The problem

Every major business disaster had warning signs.

WeWork burned through a $47 billion valuation because nobody inside the room had the mandate — or the courage — to say the obvious things out loud. The financials didn't add up. The governance was a conflict of interest factory. The competitive moat was a myth.

The same story repeats itself across failed IPOs, flawed acquisitions, and misaligned product launches: the critical questions get asked too late, or never at all.

Boards are surrounded by people who want the deal to close. Advisors who get paid when it proceeds. Teams who spent months building the plan. The incentives are all wrong.

**Red Team Agent exists to fix that.**

---

## What it does

Red Team Agent is a multi-agent AI system that reads your strategic document — an IPO filing, a business plan, an M&A memo, a product strategy — and attacks it from five adversarial angles simultaneously.

No cheerleading. No balance. Pure adversarial pressure.

In under 90 seconds, it surfaces the vulnerabilities that would take a human red team days to assemble — and that most human teams would soften anyway.

---

## The five attackers

Each agent plays a specific adversarial role, with a single-minded mandate to find what's broken:

| Agent | Role | Attack angle |
|---|---|---|
| **CFO** | The most cynical financial mind in the room | Why the numbers don't add up |
| **Market** | Your most skeptical future customer | Why nobody will actually buy this |
| **Legal** | The lawyer looking for conflicts of interest | Who really controls this, and who gets hurt |
| **Competitor** | The CEO of your main rival | How they will beat you, and why your moat is sand |
| **Execution** | The COO who's seen plans like this fail | Why this organization will never actually pull it off |

The agents work in parallel, each grounded in the actual text of your document. They don't hallucinate generic criticism — they attack the specific claims, numbers, and structures you put on paper.

---

## The output

A ranked vulnerability report with a single, unambiguous verdict:

- **Risk Score** — 0 to 100, calibrated across all findings
- **CRITICAL / HIGH / MEDIUM** findings — ordered by severity, tagged by attacker
- **Top 3 Questions** — the exact questions your board must answer before proceeding
- **Verdict** — `PROCEED`, `PROCEED WITH CAUTION`, or `DO NOT PROCEED`

No hedging. No "on the other hand." If the plan has a fatal flaw, the report says so.

---

## The demo: WeWork, 2019

WeWork filed its S-1 in August 2019 with a $47 billion valuation. Six weeks later, the IPO was withdrawn. The CEO was ousted. The company eventually went bankrupt.

The red flags were all in the document:

- Net losses exceeding total revenue
- A custom accounting metric designed to hide the burn
- A founder who had sold his own trademark back to the company
- A governance structure that made him effectively unremovable
- A "tech company" multiple applied to a real estate lease business

**Red Team Agent finds all of them in under 90 seconds.**

## The core insight

The best red teams in the world — the ones at intelligence agencies, elite investment firms, military planning rooms — operate on a simple principle: give someone the explicit job of finding failure, with no incentive to soften what they find.

AI makes it possible to do this at the speed of a document upload, with zero political friction, for any decision that matters.
